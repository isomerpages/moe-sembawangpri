<p><strong>Spotlight: Singapore Youth Festival (SYF) 2026</strong>
</p>
<p>In April, student-performers from the English Drama CCA participated in
the <strong>Singapore Youth Festival (SYF) Arts Presentation</strong>. After
months of intensive workshops and rehearsals, our student-performers delivered
a captivating performance that resonated with the audience and adjudicators
alike.</p>
<p>The SYF stage can be a daunting place to perform. Yet our students captivated
the audience with <strong>unwavering confidence and poise</strong>. Their
ability to embody complex characters and maintain stage presence under
pressure was a testament to their hard work and the collaborative spirit
of the CCA. The highlight of the performance was certainly seeing the student-performers
supporting and complementing one another on and off the stage.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Why Join English Drama?</strong>
</p>
<p>Drama is more than just acting; it is a holistic journey of self-discovery.
Student-performers in our CCA gain invaluable life skills that extend far
beyond the theater:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Boosted Self-Confidence:</strong> Standing in front of an audience
helps CCA members find their voice and overcome inferiority such as public
speaking and stage fright.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Effective Communication:</strong> CCA members learn the nuances
of verbal and non-verbal communication, improving their public speaking
and articulation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Collaboration &amp; Teamwork:</strong> Production is a massive
puzzle. Whether as an actor or a crew member, CCA members learn the importance
of responsibility, teamwork and resilience as they work toward a shared
vision and goal.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Empathy and Critical Thinking:</strong> By stepping into the shoes
of different characters, student-performers develop a better and deeper
understanding of diverse perspectives and regulating human emotions.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Creative Problem Solving:</strong> From improvising a missed line
to managing stage props, drama inculcates in student-performers the importance
of adaptability to think and stay calm under pressure.</p>
</li>
</ul>
