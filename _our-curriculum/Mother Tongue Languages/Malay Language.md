<h3>Malay Language</h3>
<p><strong>Malay Language Unit</strong>
<br><strong>Our Beliefs About Malay Language Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>We believe that every student can be nurtured into an effective communicator
and self-directed learner who is passionate about the Malay Language, appreciates
their cultural heritage, and uses the language confidently in authentic
everyday contexts.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>The Core Curriculum</strong>
</p>
<p>The Malay Language Curriculum comprises teaching and learning experiences
that promote an active and engaging learning environment. The strategies
employed are designed to encourage students to explore language concepts
in authentic and meaningful contexts, with an emphasis on interactive and
communicative competencies. Through collaborative discussions, inquiry-based
learning, and reflective practices, students are nurtured to develop E21CC
competencies such as effective communication, self-directed learning, critical
thinking, and social awareness.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Recognising that every student learns differently, differentiated instruction
is incorporated through activities and exercises that cater to students’
interests, readiness levels, and aptitudes. Formative assessment practices
are also integrated to monitor students’ progress, encourage reflection
on learning, and support the development of confident and responsible learners.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Key Programmes</strong>
</p>
<p>The Core Curriculum is further enhanced through key programmes that foster
a conducive environment for the development of language skills in a fun
and exploratory manner. These programmes embody our belief that the Malay
Language is a living language — one that is used meaningfully in everyday
communication across various contexts by students who are articulate, confident,
and passionate about their Mother Tongue Language. Through authentic and
meaningful learning experiences, students are also provided with opportunities
to develop E21CC competencies such as communication, collaboration, cultural
awareness, and self-directed learning.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Key Programmes in SBPS are:</p>
<p><strong>MTL SOAR Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>The MTL SOAR Programme aims to nurture students’ love for reading in the
Mother Tongue Language through meaningful and enjoyable reading experiences.
Through the programme, students discover that reading can be a “seated
adventure” — sparking creativity and imagination, broadening perspectives,
opening pathways to new discoveries, and exposing them to rich vocabulary
and expressions.</p>
<p>Through activities such as individual reading, storytelling, role play,
book discussions, and shared reading experiences with peers and family
members, students develop positive reading habits and confidence as bilingual
readers. The programme also fosters a strong culture of reading beyond
the classroom while strengthening E21CC competencies such as communication,
self-directed learning, and critical thinking.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Reading Mum Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>The Reading Mum Programme provides additional support for students who
require assistance in developing their Mother Tongue Language reading skills.
Conducted during curriculum hours, the programme provides students with
dedicated reading support in a caring and encouraging environment through
the support of parent volunteers. During the 30-minute sessions, students
engage in guided reading activities that help to build confidence, strengthen
literacy skills, and cultivate positive reading habits. The programme also
fosters meaningful school-home partnerships while nurturing students to
become more confident and independent readers.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Authentic Communication Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>This programme provides Primary 5 students with opportunities to use the
Malay Language in authentic and meaningful contexts through digital and
media-based learning experiences. They engage in activities such as creating
vlogs, sharing reviews, and promoting products or experiences in the style
of digital influencers. Through these tasks, students develop confidence,
creativity, and communicative competencies while strengthening E21CC skills
such as effective communication, critical thinking, collaboration, and
self-directed learning.</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Cultural Awareness Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>The annual Cultural Camp provides Primary 3 students with an immersive
and experiential learning journey that deepens their appreciation of Malay
culture and heritage. Through authentic cultural experiences such as traditional
games, food preparation, dikir barat, and batik painting, students engage
meaningfully with the traditions and practices of the Malay community.
These experiences foster a stronger sense of cultural identity and appreciation
while nurturing E21CC competencies such as social awareness, collaboration,
and effective communication.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Cultural Performance Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>The annual Cultural Performance Programme for Primary 4 students provides
enriching learning experiences that deepen their appreciation and love
for the Malay Language. Through exposure to local Malay cultural performances,
students are immersed in authentic arts experiences beyond the classroom.
These experiences broaden their cultural understanding, strengthen their
connection to the Malay Language, and foster E21CC competencies such as
cultural awareness, reflection, and communication.</p>
