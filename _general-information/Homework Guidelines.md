<h3><strong>Homework Guidelines</strong></h3>
<p>Homework refers to any learning activity that schools require students
to complete outside of curriculum time, regardless of the mode of delivery
(i.e. pen-and-paper, virtual/ online).&nbsp; This includes assignments
and projects but not revision of school work or studying for tests and
examinations.</p>
<p>Homework, when used appropriately, can reinforce students’ learning, provide
feedback on their progress and cultivate a healthy disposition towards
continual learning.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Effective use of homework can:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; reinforce learning and deepen understanding
of lessons taught;</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; allow students to regularly practise,
and apply skills and concepts taught;</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; develop good study skills and habits;
and</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; encourage greater ownership and
responsibility for independent learning.</p>
<p>Homework also provides parents with:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; timely information on the learning
progress of their child; and</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; opportunities to be involved in
their child’s learning.</p>
<p>For their wellbeing and holistic development, students need to have a
balanced life with adequate time for family bonding, social activities,
personal interests and rest. As such, the school takes reference from the
following guidelines when assigning homework:</p>

<p>Students will be taught and expected to</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; refer to the homework board in the
classroom daily for the homework assigned.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; record the homework assigned in
the Student Handbook.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; bring home the work assigned for
completion and bring back to school for submission.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; manage their time well to ensure
that homework is completed and submitted on time.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; review feedback from teachers on
homework submitted.</p>
<p>Parents can</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; create a home environment conducive
for studying and completion of homework;</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; supervise and provide support for
child’s learning;</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; reinforce good study habits and
attitudes;</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; be mindful of the stresses arising
from school homework and out-of-school activities, and help your child
prioritise his/her time among these activities; and</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; work in partnership with the school
and teachers to support your child’s learning and development.</p>
